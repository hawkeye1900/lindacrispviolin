It has been an absolute pleasure to be taught by Linda for 12 years since I was 6 years old, from learning to putting on a shoulder rest to currently working towards the ARSM diploma this summer. She has both an immense technical knowledge of the violin and a deep understanding of musicality and different genres of music. Her enthusiasm is apparent through her lessons and encouragement for her students to participate in all sorts of musical opportunities such as joining local orchestras, playing ensemble music with her students and taking part in competitions. Lessons are particularly engaging due to both her dedication to playing to the strengths of individual pupils and the back-and-forth aspect of our musical discussions.

I have known Linda Crisp since her student days when she had violin lessons with me at the Colchester Institute. Her passion and enthusiasm for the violin was clear to me from the outset, and she has built a professional career based on her violinistic talent and her dedication and total commitment to music and to the instrument. Linda is not only a player of distinction but also a gifted teacher of both violin and viola. Her main philosophy is firstly to the music, providing the student with the understanding of the style and the meaning of the music, then giving them the technical equipment with which to achieve these musical concepts. Linda has a most friendly and enthusiastic personality, she enjoys building a relationship with her students, assuring that the musical experience is always positive and enduring. She has extensive knowledge of violinistic techniques, and has access to a very wide range of repertoire to suit all levels and ages of students from beginners to Conservatoire entrance. She also coaches chamber ensembles, and enjoys working with amateur players and groups. Linda Crisp is a very valuable member of the teaching community.

Diana Cummings ARAM FRAM
Professor at the Royal Academy of Music and Trinity Laban Conservatoire of Music and Dance.
